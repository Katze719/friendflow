# Account Deletion

**Last updated:** June 30, 2026

friendflow accounts can be deleted from inside the app and through the web interface of the instance where the account exists. The official public friendflow instance is **https://friendflow.site/**.

## Delete your account in the app

1. Sign in to the friendflow instance where your account exists.
2. Open **Account settings**.
3. Choose **Delete account**.
4. Enter your current password and confirm deletion.

After confirmation, the account can no longer sign in. Personal-only data such as private calendar entries, private tasks, private shopping lists, password reset tokens, and connected Google Calendar tokens are removed from the instance.

## Shared group data

friendflow is built around shared groups. Content that other group members can see, such as group expenses, trip plans, tasks, events, shopping items, tournaments, and historical activity, may remain so the shared group stays usable for the remaining members. Your account identity is removed from the active member list and replaced with a deleted-user label where historical records still need a stable reference.

If you are the only member of a group, deleting your account also removes that group and its data.

## Request deletion without app access

If you cannot sign in, contact the operator of the instance where your account exists and include:

- The instance URL.
- The email address of the account.
- A short statement that you want the account deleted.

For the official public friendflow instance at **https://friendflow.site/**, use the support or privacy contact published by that instance operator.

## Backups and logs

Backups and server logs are controlled by the instance operator. Deleted account data may remain in encrypted backups or operational logs for a limited retention period before those backups expire. The operator's Privacy Policy should state the applicable retention period and contact details.

## Custom instances

If you switched the app to a custom/self-hosted instance, that instance is operated independently. Its operator controls account deletion, backups, logs, and privacy requests for accounts on that server.
